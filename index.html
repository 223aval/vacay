Staten Island

Filled with landmarks, beaches, and parkland, Staten Island has the most greenery out of the five Boroughs of New York City.

But how did we get here? What made Staten Island what it is today?

  • I. Native Americans seem to have inhabited the island about 14,000 years ago. However, they didn't stick around due to the extirpation of large animals on the island.
  • II. At the time of European conctact, the Raritan band (a subdivision of the Lenape) resided in Staten Island. They moved seasonally, relying on slash and burn agriculture and fishing.
  • III. At the end of the Second Anglo-Dutch War in 1667, the Dutch ceded New Netherland to England, and Staten Island became part of New York.
